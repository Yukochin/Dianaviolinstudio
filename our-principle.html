About Diana Chen

An award-winning violinist and educator, Ms.Diana Chen is widely recognized for her artistry, dedication, and inspiring approach to teaching. A graduate of the Yong Siew Toh Conservatory of Music, National University of Singapore, Ms.Diana Chen completed her Bachelor’s Degree in Violin Performance under a full scholarship in 2018.

During her studies, Ms.Diana Chen distinguished herself as both a soloist and chamber musician, earning numerous accolades — including 3rd Prize in the Violin Category at the Singapore Concerto Competition (2015) and 2nd Prize at the Qingyuan International Music Festival in China.

As a performing artist, Ms.Diana Chen has appeared with leading orchestras and productions such as the Singapore Lyric Opera Orchestra, Stuttgart Ballet (Romeo and Juliet), and the Metropolitan Festival Orchestra. She has also graced the stage in world-renowned musicals, including the American Broadway production of “The Phantom of the Opera” during its Singapore run.

Beyond the classical stage, Ms.Diana Chen embraces musical diversity — performing with celebrated artists like Joanna Dong and Sachiyo in cross-genre collaborations that showcase her versatility and expressive musicianship. In 2017, she had the distinct honour of performing for the President of Singapore, Mdm Halimah Yacob, at an official event.

Today, Ms.Diana Chen is the founder and principal violin instructor at Diana Violin Studio, where she guides a vibrant community of students from both local and international schools. Known for her bilingual teaching and patient, structured approach, she nurtures each student’s potential — from beginners to advanced performers — helping them grow in confidence, discipline, and musical artistry.

Her students have flourished under her guidance, winning awards in international competitions and earning admission to renowned conservatories abroad and leading local schools through the Direct School Admission (DSA) programme.

Through her studio, Ms.Diana Chen continues her lifelong mission: to inspire excellence through music, and to share the joy, artistry, and connection that the violin brings to life.

Our Teaching Principles

  • To spark curiosity and imagination through music, guiding students to think creatively and express themselves with heart.
  • To inspire independent learning through patient guidance and a supportive environment where each child feels safe to explore, grow, and discover their own musical voice.
  • To lay a strong foundation in good techniques, helping every student build confidence and joy in their playing.
  • To nurture self-discipline, perseverance, and focus while encouraging a healthy sense of self-belief and pride in every small achievement.
  • To care for every student as an individual — understanding their pace, personality, and strengths so that each one can shine in their own unique way.
